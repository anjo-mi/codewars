<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/59bb02f5623654a0dc000119/train/javascript target="_blank">Baby Magpies</a></h2><h3>6 kyu</h3><h1 id="magpies-are-my-favourite-birds">Magpies are my favourite birds</h1><p>Baby ones even more so...</p><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Magpie_samcem05.jpg/220px-Magpie_samcem05.jpg"><p>It is a little known fact^ that the black &amp; white colours of baby magpies differ by <strong>at least</strong> one place and <strong>at most</strong> two places from the colours of the mother magpie.</p><p>So now you can work out if any two magpies may be related. </p><p><em>...and Quardle oodle ardle wardle doodle the magpies said</em></p><h1 id="kata-task">Kata Task</h1><p>Given the colours of two magpies, determine if one is a possible <strong>child</strong> or <strong>grand-child</strong> of the other.</p><h1 id="notes">Notes</h1><ul><li>Each pair of birds being compared will have same number of colour areas</li><li><code>B</code> = Black</li><li><code>W</code> = White</li></ul><h1 id="example">Example</h1><p>Given these three magpies</p><pre style="font-size:6em;line-height:90px;"><span style="font-size:20px;">Magpie 1  </span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="font-size:20px;">Magpie 2  </span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:black;color:brown">B</span><span style="font-size:20px;">Magpie 3  </span><span style="background:white;color:brown">W</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="background:white;color:brown">W</span><span style="background:white;color:black">W</span><span style="background:black">B</span><span style="background:black">B</span></pre><p>You can see:</p><ul><li>Magpie 2 may be a child of Magpie 1 because there is only one difference</li><li>Magpie 3 may be child of Magpie 2 because there are two differences</li><li>So Magpie 3 may be a grand-child of Magpie 1</li><li>On the other hand, Magpie 3 cannot be a child of Magpie 1 because there are three differences</li></ul><hr><p>DM
